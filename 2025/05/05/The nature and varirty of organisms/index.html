The nature and variety of organisms

Posted on 2025-05-05 In IGCSE , Biology Views:

Revision note for Edexcel IGCSE Biology.

Discussing kingdoms of organisms and characteristics of them.

Characteristics of living organisms:

  • movement
    • action by an organism causing a change of position or place
    • movement from place to place: locomotion
    • plants cannot move from place to place but change their orientation.
  • respiration
    • Chemical reaction in all organisms
    • Energy is released from glucose as ATP
    • presence of oxygen: aerobic respiration
    • absence of oxygen: anaerobic respiration
    • releasing carbon dioxide & water
    • $ glucose + oxygen ; -! > carbon dioxide + water $
  • sensitivity
    • detect and respond to stimuli in the surroundings
    • increase the chances of survival
    • animals:
      • nervous system:
        • receptors
        • neurones
        • effectors
        • use electrical impulses
      • endocrine system
        • hormones
        • use chemical messengers
        • travel in the blood
    • plants:
      • controlled by chemicals
      • much slower
      • geotropism:
        • gravity
        • e.g. root - positively geotropism
      • phototropism:
        • light
        • e.g. leaf - positively phototropism
  • control
    • homeostasis
    • control internal environment in order to keep conditions within required limits
    • human:
      • body optimum temperature: 37°C
      • sweating
      • vasodilation
      • glucoregulation(blood glucose level)
      • osmoregulation(water level)
    • plants:
      • transpiration
        • water evaporates from stomata to loss heat
        • water is taken up from soil through roots
  • growth
    • permanent increase in size
    • animals
      • zygote -> adult
      • change in proportion or shape
    • plants
      • grow larger in the whole life
      • new shoots, leaves, branches
  • reproduction:
    • production of more of the same kind of organism
    • survival of species
    • sexual reproduction:
      • gametes fuse together
        • in humans: sperm + egg
        • in plants: pollen grains + ovule
    • asexual reproduction:
      • e.g. mitosis
      • only one parent -> clone
      • DNA of offspring is identical to parental DNA
      • plants + single-celled organisms(bacteria, amoeba)
      • in plants: tubers, buddings, runners
  • excretion
    • removal of toxic materials and substances from organism
    • metabolic reaction: chemical reactions that take place inside living cells
    • some of metabolic reaction waste is toxic
    • animal excretion waste:
      • carbon dioxide - respiration
      • water - respiration and others
      • urea(contain nitrogen) - break down of protein
    • plants excretion waste:
      • oxygen - photosynthesis
      • carbon dioxide - respiration
      • water - respiration and other
  • nutrition
    • obtain food for energy.
    • Plants(autotrophic): photosynthesis.
    • animals(heterotrophic): digest food from a range of different sources.

    • -Autotroph make their own food
      -Heterotroph get food from a range of food sources

Eukaryotic organisms:

Basics

  • five kingdoms of eukaryotes:
    • animals
    • plants
    • fungi
    • protoctists
    • Bacteria
  • features:
    • multicellular or unicellular
    • contain a nucleus with distinct membranes in each cell
  • standard animal cells:
organelle function
nucleus -contain genetic material(DNA)
-controls the activities of the cell(by DNA)
cytoplasm -gel-like substances
-consist of water and dissolved solutes
-support internal cell structures
-site of many chemical reactions (e.g. anaerobic respiration)
-contains enzymes which control these reactions
cell membrane -separating the inside of cells from outside
-controls enter and leave of substances to the cell
ribosome -found in the cytoplasm
-site of protein synthesis
mitochondria -site of aerobic respiration
-release energy to fuel cellular process
-cells with higher rate of metabolism reactions have higher number of mitochondria
  • plant cells’ specific organelles:
organelle function
cell wall -made by cellulose (glucose polymer)
-extra support to the cell; defining shape
chloroplast -contain chlorophyll(green) to absorb light energy
-contain enzymes needed for photosynthesis
permanent vacuole -contain cell sap - solution of sugar and salts (juice)
-store certain materials
-support the shape of the cell

Animals

  • multicellular
  • cells contain nuclei with distinct membranes
  • no cellulose cell walls
  • no chloroplast(photosynthesis)
  • heterotrophic
  • usually have nervous coordination
  • can move from place to place
  • store carbohydrates as glycogen

Plants

  • multicellular
  • cells contain nuclei with distinct membranes
  • cells contain cell walls (made by cellulose)
  • contain chloroplasts (photosynthesis)
  • autotrophic (feed by photosynthesis)
  • store carbohydrates as starch or sucrose
  • no nervous coordination

Fungi

  • usually multicellular; some are unicellular(e.g.yeast)
    • multicellular fungi are made up of hyphae containing many nuclei
    • are organised into a network - mycelium
  • have nucleus with distinct membrane
  • no chloroplast(no photosynthesis) - heterotrophic
  • contain cell walls (made by chitin)
  • store carbohydrates as glycogen
  • saprotrophic nutrition
    • feed by secreting extracellular digestive enzymes(outside of mycelium)
    • absorb the digested molecules

Protoctists

  • unicellular or multicellular
  • have nucleus with distinct membrane
  • some have chloroplast - autotrophic
  • some have cell walls
  • store carbohydrates as glycogen
  • no nervous coordination

Prokaryotic organisms:

features:

  • always single-celled
  • do not contain a nucleus
  • have nuclear material in cytoplasm in prokaryotic cells
  • smaller than eukaryotic cells

Bacteria

  • unicellular microscopic organisms
  • no nucleus (but contain circular DNA)
  • contain cell walls
  • no mitochondria
  • no chloroplast - heterotrophic and autotrophic
  • store carbohydrates as glycogen or PHB
