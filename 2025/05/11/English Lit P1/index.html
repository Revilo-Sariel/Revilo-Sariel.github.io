Paper 1 section A -writing a response

Posted on 2025-05-11 Edited on 2025-05-12 In IGCSE , English Lit

Revision note for CIE English Lit Paper 1 section A.

Including structure and language techniques and answering tips.

Section A

answering tips

time management

time spend task
10 min - decide question
- highlight keywords on the question
- re-read the poem
- annotate
- make brief plan of answering structure
30 min - follow the plan and annotations
- write response
5 min - re-read and check answers

Key words

  • explore: write in detail
  • how: examine techniques the writer has used to achieve a specific effect
  • vividly / convey: produce powerful feelings and strong clear images in mind
  • how far / to what extent: an element of the text allowing to make a counter-argument
  • portray: explore how writer describe or bring a character or a theme to life in text.
  • memorable: how writer presents a character of event in a way that stands out / worth remembering

responses tips

  • highlight keywords in the questions
  • use quotations to support the idea, themselves should not be a point of view
  • respond to every section of the poem
  • last stanza:
    • where poet emphasises their message or attitude
  • give reasons for the explanation and annotations
    • because, as, since
  • link to the questions
  • embed the quotations into the sentence
    • this is shown by quote …
  • avoid narratives
  • include annotation of structure and form
  • conclusion of response:
    • personal response on the poem and questions
    • give new point of views

structure and forms of poems

Forms / genre

  • sonnet
    • 14 lines
    • usually theme of love
    • petrarchan sonnet(Italian)
      • ABBA, ABBA, CDE, CDE rhyme scheme
    • shakespearean sonnet(English)
      • ABAB, CDCD, EFEF, GG rhyme scheme
  • epic
    • ancient
    • lengthy narrative poem
    • typically heroic deeds
  • free verse
    • a lack of rules
    • can rhyme or not
    • about any themes
  • villanelle
    • 19 lines
    • five stanza of 3 lines
    • final stanza of 4 lines
    • only 2 rhyming sounds(ABA)
  • ode
    • originally performed with musical instruments
    • praise a person, event or thing
    • short in length
  • ballad
    • telling dramatic or emotional story
    • ABAB or ABCB rhyme scheme

poets may alter the rhyme scheme, subverting a traditional form to convey an idea

structure

techniques explanation
stanza length changes on the length of lines
repetition repetition of words, phrase or ideas
caesura using punctuations at the mid of a line
enjambment a lack of punctuation at the end of a line
shift change in ideas and perspectives
contrast difference between two things
pace the feeling of speed in the writing
temporal references references to time
dialogue conversations and speech

Language

Terms of language

  • comparative adjectives
    • used to compare difference between 2 nouns
    • e.g. larger, smaller, faster
  • superlatives
    • used to express something of the highest quality of its kind
    • e.g. fairest, prettiest, funest
  • emotive language
    • words and phrases that are used to evoke strong emotional responses in the reader
  • colloquial language
    • informal, everyday language
  • imperatives
    • verb forms or sentences that are used to give commands
    • e.g. “Fill your belly”, “Day and night make merry”

language techniques

  • simile
    • directly compare two different things
    • use “like”,“as”
  • metaphor
    • describe one thing in terms of another
    • indirectly compare
  • imagery
    • engage the readers senses by using vivid and detailed language to create mental image
  • alliteration
    • repetition of consonant sounds
    • e.g. beech buggy
  • personification
    • give objects human characteristics or movements
  • onomatopoeia
    • words that imitate or mimic the sounds
  • repetition
    • repeat of words, phrases, sounds or structure to create emphasis
  • juxtaposition
    • contrast
    • place two contrasting things side by side to highlight the differences
  • assonance
    • repetition of vowel sounds
    • sounds similar
    • e.g. moonlit pool, cock of the walk
  • rule of three
    • list of 3 things or ideas
    • e.g. freedom, equality and justice
  • rhetorical questions
    • a question asked for effect with no answer expected
  • emotive language
    • words or phrases that can bring an emotional response
  • hyperbole
    • exaggeration for effect
