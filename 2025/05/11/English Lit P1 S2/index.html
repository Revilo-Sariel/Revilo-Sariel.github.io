Paper 1 section B -writing a response

Posted on 2025-05-11 Edited on 2025-05-12 In IGCSE , English Lit

Revision note for CIE English Lit Paper 1 section B.

Including terms and answering strategies.

Section B

preparing response

read questions:

Choose 1 question from 2 question choices

  • question 1
    • giving a passage from a text
  • question 2
    • no passage, whole text

time management

time spend task
10 min - decide question
- highlight keywords on the question
- annotate keywords and phrases
- make brief plan of answering structure
30 min - follow the plan and annotations
- write response
5 min - re-read and check answers

responses tips

  • highlight keywords in the questions
  • use quotations to support the idea, themselves should not be a point of view
  • respond to every section of the poem
  • last stanza:
    • where poet emphasises their message or attitude
  • give reasons for the explanation and annotations
    • because, as, since
  • link to the questions
  • embed the quotations into the sentence
    • this is shown by quote …
  • avoid narratives
  • include annotation of structure and form
  • conclusion of response:
    • personal response on the poem and questions
    • give new point of views

analyze prose

terms

  • foreshadowing
    • a warning or a prediction of a future event in the story
    • Or employ dramatic irony
      • readers are aware of what is coming
      • characters are not
  • flashbacks
    • a scene set in a time earlier than the main story
    • be used to convey extra information about plot or character
  • soliloquies
    • a speech which a character makes to themselves
    • showing a character’s inner thoughts
  • dialogue
    • conversation between two or more characters

perspectives

  • first-person narrator
    • story is told from the perspective of a character
    • identified by the use of “I”
    • make readers feel sympathy for the character
    • Or exposing the character as an unreliable narrator
    • dramatic irony
      • the readers know something the narrator does not know
  • third-person narrator
    • story is told from the perspective that sits outside of the world of the story
    • omniscient narrator:
      • the narrator knows everything about the characters and events

writing response

introduction

  • use the wording of the question to develop an introduction in your own words

main paragraphs

  1. start with a topic sentence
  2. use quotations and references to prove the point
  3. explain how the evidence proves the point

question 1 strategies (passage)

  1. detailed analysis of the passage
  2. link to the question
  3. comment on how the passage relates to the whole text

question 2 strategies (whole text)

  1. discuss the structure and perspectives of the novel, linking to the question
  2. zoom in on particular parts of the prose which are directly relevant to the question

conclusion / summary

summarize findings and response
give new point of views(no repetition of the idea)
