Cell Structure

Posted on 2025-09-25 Edited on 2025-09-26 In Alevel , Biology

Revision note for AQA biology Alevel Topic 1.

explaining organelles in cells.

Cell Structure

cell wall

  • support & protection
  • prevention from bursting
  • material
    • cellulose = plant
    • chitin = fungi
    • peptidoglycan = bacteria

cell surface membrane

  • bilayer of phospholipid
    • polar hydrophilic phosphate head
    • non-polar hydrophobic fatty acid tail
  • controls substances entry / exit

nucleus

  • contain DNA & RNA
  • make ribosomes & rRNA
  • nuclear pore:
    • allow mRNA movement
  • chromatin:
    • DNA + histone proteins
  • nuclear envelope:
    • double membrane
    • outer connects with RER

mitochondria

  • aerobic respiration
    • ATP production
      • small soluble molecule
      • release energy by hydrolysis reaction
    • take place in matrix & cristae
  • contain DNA & 70s ribosomes
  • bilayer
    • protect the matrix (inner membrane)
  • matrix
    • interior of the mitochondria
    • contain enzymes in solution

plasmodesmata

  • pore like tubes
  • link adjacent plant cells
  • contain fine stands of cytoplasm
  • lined with the cell membrane
  • controls the movement of molecules by the shape
  • symplast pathway
    • water movement between cells via the plasmodesmata

chloroplast

  • bilayer
  • carry photosynthesis
  • stroma:
    • site of light-independent stage of photosynthesis
    • circular DNA
    • 70s ribosomes
    • enzymes
  • thylakoids:
    • site of light-dependent reactions
    • large surface area

microtubules

  • hollow tubes made of tubulin protein
  • make up the cytoskeleton
    • support and movement of the cell

centriole

  • contain 9 microtubules
  • help chromosomes separate during cell division
  • production of cilia & flagella
  • centrosome
    • centrioles lie together at right angle

cilia

  • membrane-bound
  • microtubule-containing
  • flexible
  • types of cilia
    • primary cilia
      • sensory function
    • motile cilia
      • movement of organisms or substances
      • e.g. trachea

golgi apparatus

  • a stack of curved membranes
  • consist of a group of stacked, thin, fluid filled membranes.
  • near the nucleus
  • “post office of the cell”
    • Cis face: receives proteins and lipids from other organelles.
    • Cisternae & inner lumen: packaging the lipids and proteins
      • Golgi vesicles:
        • special membrane bound parcels where proteins and lipids are packaged.
    • Trans face:
      • release the proteins or lipids after package
  • make lysosomes

lysosome

  • round membrane bound organelles
    • No distinct internal structure
    • A type of golgi vesicle
  • contain lysozymes
    • A digestive enzyme
      • Digest materials in the cell
    • A hydrolytic enzyme
      • Facilitate hydrolysis reactions
  • cellular “digestive systems”
    • Contain hydrolytic enzymes
    • Can break down macromolecules (include: proteins, carbohydrates, lipids)
  • be used to digest invading cells & digest worn out cell structures
    • Mechanisms of defense against infections
  • excrete digested material

Smooth ER (smooth endoplasmic reticulum)

  • no ribosomes (“smooth”)
  • in the cytoplasm of eukaryotic cells
  • extends from the nuclear envelope towards the membrane
  • functions:
    • synthesis lipids
      • happen inside the cisternae
      • e.g. cholesterol, steroids hormone
    • detoxify toxins
      • in the liver
    • major storage site for calcium ions

Rough ER (Rough endoplasmic reticulum)

  • the surface is covered in ribosomes
  • contain stacked, fluid-filled membranes
  • fold and package proteins -> send them to the golgi apparatus

ribosome

  • small organelles
    • free in the cytoplasm
    • attached to the Rough ER
  • site of protein synthesis
  • formed from rRNA and proteins
  • mRNA sits between the two subunits
  • no membrane
  • composed of a small subunit & large subunit
    • large subunit
      • site of translation
  • type of ribosomes:
    • 80s
      • 60s + 40s
      • in eukaryotic cells
    • 70s
      • 50s + 30s
      • in prokaryotic cells
      • in mitochondria and chloroplasts of eukaryotic cells

vacuole

  • membranous sacs
  • store cell sap
    • water
    • nutrients
    • waste materials
  • maintain cell pressure and structure
  • tonoplast
    • single membrane of vacuole
