reasoning exercise vocabulary

发表于 2024-06-25 更新于 2024-07-08 阅读次数：

Week 1

Daffodil(n.):
a tall yellow spring shaped like a trumpet.
Related vocabulary: Wales, daffodil lament, blooming.

Obtuse(adj.):
1.slow or unwilling to understand sth.
2.Obtuse angle: over-180-degree angles
Related vocabulary: smart, right angle, acute angle

Saucepan(n.):
a deep round metal pot with a lid and one long handle or two short handles.(used for cook things over heat)
Related vocabulary: stewpot, pan.

Pastry(n.):
1.A mixture of flour, fat and water or milk that is rolled out flat and baked as a base or covering for pies.
2.A small cake made using pastry
Related vocabulary: cake, cupcake.

Pyramid(n.):
1.A large building with a square or triangular base and a sharp top in ancient Egypt.
2.A solid shape with a square or triangular base and sloping side that meet in a point at the top.
3.An object or a pile of things that has the shape of a pyramid.
Related vocabulary: pyramidal, Pharaoh

Pause(n./v)
1.to stop talking or doing something before continuing.
2.A period of time during which sb stops talking or stops what they are doing.
3.A control that allows you to stop a tape recorder, CD, etc;
Related vocabulary: continue, break.

Lukewarm(adj)
1.Slightly warm;
2.Not interested or enthusiastic
Related vocabulary: uninterested, chilly, lukewarmness.

Idle(adj/v)
1.Not working hard
2.(of machines, factories,etc)Not in use
3.Without work
4.Without particular purpose or effect; useless
5.Not spent doing work or sth
Related vocabulary: lazy, unemployed, idleness

Intend(v)
1.To have a plan, resault or purpose inyour mind when you do sth
2.To plan that sth should have a particular meaning.
Related vocabulary: mean, intention.

Refuse(v,n)
1.To say that you will not do sth that sb has asked you to do
2.Waste materials that has been thrown away
Related vocabulary: turn down, deny, garbage

Nasty(adj)
1.Very bad or unpleasant
2.dangerous or serious
3.Offensive, in bad taste
Related vocabulary: mean, unkind, pleasant

Concord(n)
1.peace and agreement
2.The fact of having the same number, gender or person
Related vocabulary: harmony, discord, agreement

Tranquillity(n)
A state of peace and quiet
Related vocabulary: quiet, repose

Serenity(n)
The state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled
Related vocabulary: serenities.

Maim(v)
To injure sb seriously, causing permanent damage to their body.
Related vocabulary: disable, maimed

Cripple(v/n)
1.To damage sb’s body so that they are no longer able to walk or move normally
2.To serious damage or harm ab/sth
3.A person who is unable to walk or move normally because of a disease or injury.
Related vocabulary: break, lameness

Heal(v)
1.To become healthy again; to make sth healthy again
2.To cure sb who is ill; to make sb feel happy again
3.To put an end to sth or make sth easier to bear;
Related vocabulary: healing(adj), healer.

Espy(v)
To see sb/sth suddenly
Related vocabulary: spy, spot

Blemish(n/v)
1.A mark on the skin or on an object that spoils it look less beautiful or perfect
2.To spoil sth that is beautiful or perfect in all other ways
Related vocabulary: defect, soil, mud, spot

Sting(v/n)
1.To touch your skin or make a very small hole in it so that you feel a sharp pain
2.To feel, or to make sb feel a sharp pain in a part of their body
3.To make sb feel angry or upset.
4.To charge sb more money than they expected;
5.The sharp pointed part of an insect or creature that can go into the skin leaving a small, painful wound.
6.Any sharp pain in your body or mind
7.A clever secret plan by the police to catch criminals.
8.A clever plan by criminals to cheat people out of a lot of money.
Related vocabulary: stimulus, stick, pink, bite

Chic(adj)
Very fashionable and elegant
Related vocabulary: stylishness, fashion

Dowdy(adj)
1.not attractive or fashionable
2.Dull or boring and not attractive
Related vocabulary: ancient, callet.

Toss(v)
1.To throw sth lightly or carelessly
2.To move your head suddenly upward, especially to show that you are annoyed or impatient.
3.To move or make sb/sth move from side to side or up and down
4.To shake or turn food in order to cover it with oil, butter, etc
5.To throw a coin in the air in order to decide sth, especially by guessing which side is facing upward when it lands. (toss sb for sth)
6.An act of throwing a coin in the air in order to decide sth
7.An act of move your head suddenly upward, especially to show that you are annoyed or impatient.
8.An act of throwing sth, especially in a competition or game
Related vocabulary: cast, wap, wallow

Fling(v,n)
1.to throw sb/sth somewhere with force, especially because you are angry.
2.To move yourself or part of your body suddenly and with a lot of force
3.To say sth to sb in an aggressive way
4.A short period of enjoyment when you do not allow yourself to worry or think seriously about anything
Related vocabulary: put, cast, hurtle

Bashful(adj)
Shy and easily embarrassed
Related vocabulary: shy, blate

Wary(adj)
Careful when dealing with sb/sth because you think that there may be a danger or problem
Related vocabulary: cautious, prudent

Recorder(n)
1.A machine for recording sound or pictures or both.
2.A musical instrument in the shape of a pipe that you blow into, with holes that you cover with your fingers.
3.A person who keeps a record of events or facts.
Related vocabulary: tape recorder

Promptly(adv)
1.Without delay
2.Exactly at the correct time or at the time mentioned
3.Immediately(always used before the verb)
Related vocabulary: punctually, quickly, rapidly.

Bail(n/v)
1.money that sb agrees to pay if a person accused of a crime does not appear at their trial. When bail has been arranged, the accused person is allowed to go free until the trial.
2.To release sb on bail
3.To leave a place, especially quickly.
4.To approach sb and talk to them, often when they do not want this.

Week 2

Squally(adj)
Involving sudden, violent and strong winds(weather)
Related vocabulary: showers, squall

Sultry(adj)
1.Very hot and uncomfortable
2.Sexually attractive(a woman or her appearance)
Related vocabulary: muggy, sexy

Blizzard(n)
1.A snowstorm with very strong winds
2.A large quantity of things that may seem to be attracting you.

Palm(n/v)
1.The inner surface of the hand between the wrist and the fingers
2.A straight tree with a mass of long leaves at the top, growing in the tropical countries.
3.To hide a coin, card, etc. In your hand, especially when performing a trick.
Related vocabulary: vola

Decade(n)
1.A period of ten years, especially a period such as 1990-1999
Related vocabulary: decennium, dix

Sleet(n/v)
1.A mixture of rain and snow.
2.When it is sleeting, a mixture of rain and snow is falling from the sky.
Related vocabulary: verglas, sleety

Ballad(n)
1.A song or poem that tells a story
2.A slow popular song about love
Related vocabulary: derry, volkslied, balladeer

Elect(v,sdj)
1.To choose sb to do a particular job by voting for them
2.To choose to do sth
3.Used after nouns show that sb has been chosen for a job, but is not yet doing that job.(e.g.the president elect)
4.People who have been chosen to be saved from punishment after death.
Related vocabulary: remarkable, choose

Penitent(adj/ n)
1.Feeling or showing that you are sorry for having done sth wrong
2.A person who shows that they are sorry for doing sth wrong, especially a religious person who wants God to forgive
Related vocabulary: confessional, confessor, penitence

Bill(n/v)
1.A piece of paper that shows how much you owe sb for goods or services
2.A piece of paper that shows how much you have to pay for the food and drinks that you had in a restaurant.
3.To send sb a bill for sth
4.To advertise or describe sb/sth in a particular way
5.To advertise that sb/sth will do sth.
Related vocabulary: statement, invoice

Adore(v)
1.To love sb very much
2.To like sth very much
Related vocabulary: love, sweetheart.

Sage(n/adj )
1.A plant with flat light green leaves that have a strong smell and are used in cooking as a herb
2.A very wise person
3.Wise, especially because you have a lot of experience
Related vocabulary: herb, philosopher,

Ponder(v)
To think about sth carefully for a period of time
Related vocabulary: consider.

Mend(v/n)
1.To repair sth that has been damaged or broken so that it can be used again
2.To find a solution to a problem or disagreement
3.To improve in health after being ill/sick
4.To heal(of a broken bone)
Related vocabulary: recover, improve, modify

Rear(n/adj/v)
1.The back part of sth
2.The part of the body that you sit on
3.At or near the back of sth
4.To care for young children or animals until they are fully grown
5.To breed or keep animals or birds, for example on a farm
6.To raise itself on its back legs, with the front legs in the air
7.To seem to lean over you, especially in a threatening way(sth large)
Related vocabulary: raise, back, aback, set

Week 3

Sombre(adj)
1.dark in colour, dull
2.Sad and serious
Related vocabulary: drab, melancholy, upset, dark

Daybreak(n)
The time of day when light first appears
Related vocabulary: dawn

Govern( adj )
1.To legally control a country or its people and be responsible for introducing new laws, organizing public services, etc
2.To control or influence sb/sth or how sth happens, function, etc
3.If a word governs another word or phrase, it affects how that word or phrase is formed or used.
Related vocabulary: manage, possess, conduct, regulate.

Timid(adj)
Shy and nervous; not brave
Related vocabulary: modest, harehearted

Brisk(adj)
1.Quick, busy
2.Practical and confident; showing a desire to get things done quickly
3.Cold but pleasantly fresh
Related vocabulary: alive, acute, keen, lively, activated

Bold(adj)
1.Brave and confident, not afraid to say what you feel or to take risks
2.That can be easily seen; having a strong clear appearance
Related vocabulary: rapid , stouthearted

Operate(v)
1.To work in a particular way
2.To be used or working; to use sth or make it work
3.to cut open sb’s body in order to remove a part that has a disease or to repair a part that is damaged
4.To be involved in military activities in a place
Related vocabulary: act, handle, operation

Counterfeit(adj/v)
1.Made to look exactly like sth in order to trick people into thinking that they are getting the real thing
2.To make an exact copy of sth in order to trick people into thinking that it is the real thing
Related vocabulary: fake, simulate, shoddy, counterfeiter

Immerse(v)
1.To put sb/sth into a liquid so that they or it are completely covered
2.To become or make sb completely involved in sth
Related vocabulary: entrap

Contort(v)
To become twisted or make sth twisted out of its natural or normal shape
Related vocabulary: distort\

Panorama(n)
1.a view of a wide area of land
2.A description, study or set of pictures that presents all the different aspects or stages of a particular subject, event, etc
Related vocabulary: panoramic, panoptic

Conceit(n)
1.Too much pride in yourself and what you do
2.An artistic effect or device, especially one that is very clever or tries to be very clever but does not succeed
3.A clever expression in writing or speech that involves a comparison between two things
Related vocabulary: ego, fantasize

Week 4

Overcast(adj ):
Covered with clouds;dull
Related vocabulary: cloudy, dull,shadow, shield

Kindle(v)
1.To start burning; to make a fire start burning
2.To make sth such as an interest, emotion, etc. Start to grow in sb; to start to be felt by sb
Related vocabulary: lamp, emblaze

Potent(adj)
1.Having a strong effect on your body or mind
2.Powerful
Related vocabulary: valid, effective, virtual

Scrub(v/n)
1.To clean sth by rubbing it hard, perhaps with a brush and usually with soap and water
2.To cancel sth that you have arranged to do
3.An act of scrubbing
4.Small bushes and trees
5.The special clothes worn by surgeons when they are doing medical operations
Related vocabulary: small, scouring, depurate

Anticipate(v)
1.To expect sth
2.To see what might happen in the future and take action to prepare for it
3.To think with pleasure and excitement about sth that is going to happen
4.To do sth before it an be done by sb else
Related vocabulary: hope, expect

Gossip(n/v)
1.Informal talk or stories about other people’s private lives, that may be unkind or not true
2.A conversation about other people
3.A person who enjoys talking about other people’s private lives
Related vocabulary: essay, quidnunc

Impede(v)
To delay or stop the process of sth
Related vocabulary: hinder, hamper

Agile(adj)
1.Able to move quickly and easily
2.Able to think quickly and in an intelligent way
Related vocabulary: nimble, fly, sharp , keen

Enclosure(n)
1.A piece of land that is surrounded by a fence or wall and is used for a particular purpose
2.The act of placing a fence or wall around a piece of land
3.Something that is placed in an envelope with a letter
Related vocabulary: attachment, pen

Week 5

Apparel(u):
1.Clothing, when it is being sold in shops/stores
2.Clothes, particularly those worn on a formal occasion
Related vocabulary: clothing, garment, array, wear.

Bleach(v/n):

  1. to make sth white or pale by a chemical process or by the effect of light from the sun; to become white or pale in this way
    3.A chemical that is used to make sth become white or pale and as a disinfectant.
    Related vocabulary: snow, whiten, decolorant, whitener.

Emerge(v)
1.To come out of a dark, confined or hidden place
2.To become known
3.To start to exist; to appear or become known
4.To survive a difficult situation or experience
Related vocabulary: appear.

Blend(v/n)
1.To mix two or more substances together
2.To form a mixture with sth
3.To combine with sth in an attractive or effective way; to combine sth in this way
4.To produce sth by mixing different types together
5.A mixture of different types of the same thing
6.A pleasant or useful combination of different things(a blend of youth and experience 年轻而且有经验)
Related vocabulary: compound, mixture, sort

Week 6

Solitary(adj/n)
1.Done alone; without other people
2.Enjoying being alone; frequently spending time alone
3.Alone with no other people or things around
4.Only one(in negative sentences and questions)
Related vocabulary: alone, neighborless, hermit, anchorite, solitarily, solitude, solitariness

Plaice 比目鱼

Hake 鳕鱼

Sole 可食用比目鱼

Lamb(n/v)
1.A young sheep
2.Meat from a young sheep
3.Used to describe or address sb with affection or pity
4.To give birth to a lamb
Related vocabulary: kidlet, yeanling, lamblike

Veal(n)
Meat from a calf(a young cow)
Related vocabulary: calf, runt

Foe(n)
An enemy
Related vocabulary: enemy, anti, opponent

Adversary(n)
A person that sb is opposed to and competing with in and argument or a battle
Related vocabulary: equal, opponent, comparative, rival, match

Novel(n/adj)
1.A story long enough to fill a complete book,in which the characters and events are usually imaginary
2.Different from anything known before; new, interesting and often seeming slightly strange
Related vocabulary: patent, exquisite, story, fiction

burst in on sb/sth:
to interrupt sb/sth by entering a place suddenly and noisily
Related: barge

Pallid(adj)
1.Pale, especially because of illness
2.Not strong or bright, and therefore not attractive
Related vocabulary: gray, dirty, pale, wan

Faint(adj/v/n)
1.That cannot be clearly seen, heard or smelt
2.Very small; possible but unlikely
3.Not enthusiastic
4.Feeling weak and tired and likely to become unconscious
5.To become unconscious when not enough blood is going to your brain, usually because of the heat, a shock, etc.
6.The state of becoming unconscious
Related vocabulary: fuzzy, weak, dark, black out.

Conceal(v)
To hide sb/sth
Related vocabulary: lock, pocket, to hide

Week 7

Lettuce(n)
A plant with large green leaves that are eaten raw, especially in salad. There are many types of lettuce
Related vocabulary: william, banknote

Effortless(adj)
Needing little or no effort, so that it seems easy
Related vocabulary: easy, light, easiest

Diverse(adj)
Very different from each other and of various kinds
Different, unlike, varying, another, dissimilar

Curtail(v)
To limit sth or make it last for a shorter time
Related vocabulary: crop, to cut back

Stretch(v/n)
1.To make sth longer, wider or looser, for example by pulling it; to become longer, etc. In this way
2.To become bigger or longer when you pull it and return to its original shape when you stop
3.To pull sth so that it is smooth and tight
4.To put you arms or legs out straight and contract you muscles
5.To put out an arm or a leg in order to reach sth
6.To spread over an area of land
7.To continue over a period of time
8.To be enough to buy or pay for sth
9.To make use of a lot of your money, supplies, time, etc.
10.To make use of all sb’s skill, intelligence, etc.
11.To use sth in a way that would not normally be considered fair, acceptable, etc
12.An area of land or water, especially a long one
13.A continuous period of time
14.A period of time that sb spends in prison
15.An act of stretching out your arms or legs or your body and contracting the muscles; the state of being stretched
16.The ability to be made longer or wider without breaking or tearing
17.A straight part at the end of a racing track
Related vocabulary: lengthen

Tardy(adj)
Slow to act, move or happen; late in happening or arriving
Related vocabulary: slow-motion, overdue, late for, late arrival.

Punctual(adj)
Happening or doing sth at the arranged or correct time, not late
Related vocabulary: accurate, precise, mathematical, strict, rigid

Cradle(n/v)
1.A small bed for a baby which can be pushed gently from side to side
2.The place where sth important began
3.A small platform that can be moved up and down the outside of a high building, used by people cleaning windows, etc
4.The part of a telephone on which the receiver rests
5.To hold sb/sth gently in your arms or hands
Related vocabulary: cot, bassinet, foster

Crib(n/v)
1.=cot, cradle
2.A long open box that horses and cows can eat from
3.Written information such as an answer to questions, often used dishonestly by students in tests
4.The house, flat/apartment, etc.
5.To dishonestly copy work from another student or from a book
Related vocabulary: cot, cradle, copy, plagiarize

Hamper(n/v)
1.To prevent sb from easily doing or achieving sth
2.A large basket with a lid, a box or package containing food, sent as a gift
3.A large basket that you keep your dirty clothes in until they are washed
Related vocabulary: let, chain, hinder, slow down, hindrance, basket, punnet

Bazaar(n)
1.(in some Eastern countries)A street or an area of a town where there are many small shops
2.(in british, the US, etc) a sale of goods, often items made by hand, to raise money for a charity
Related vocabulary: exchanges, mart, plaza

Nasty(adj)
1.Very bad or unpleasant
2.Unkind; unpleasant
3.Dangerous or serious
4.Offensive; in bad taste
Related vocabulary: blue, dirty

Crown(n/v)
1.An object in the shape of a circle, usually made of gold and precious stones, that a king or queen wears on his or her head on official occasions
2.The highest part of sth
3.to put a crown on the head of a new king or queen as a sign of royal power
4.To form or cover the top of sth
5.To hit sb on the head
Related vocabulary: height, summit, peak, coronate, cite
